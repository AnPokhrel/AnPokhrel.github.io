Current Projects

Metagenomics for source attribution of foodborne pathogens: In my current part of broad PhD project, I am assessing metagenomic sequencing for characterisation and source attribution of foodborne pathogens, with a particular focus on Salmonella enterica. Using a spiked-in experimental setup, the study employs shotgun metagenomics and a suite of bioinformatics tools to assess bacterial community dynamics, detect antimicrobial resistance genes, and perform high-resolution subtyping through SNPs and cgMLST. The project integrates structural, and functional metagenomics with machine learning approaches to evaluate how effectively metagenomics can detect, type, and trace pathogens implicated in food outbreak, ultimately contributing to improved public health surveillance and outbreak response.

In later part of my PhD project, I will be leveraging metagenomics for AMR detection in environmental sources like beach water.
